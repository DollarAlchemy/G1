Codex Vitae

"A Codex Vitae — a book of life — is not a diary. It is a record of what you believe to be true, what you have found to work, and what you are still figuring out." — Opening Entry

The Codex Vitae is the second volume of this grimoire. Where Systemcraft concerns the logic of machines and craft, the Codex Vitae concerns the logic of a life — how to think, how to direct attention, how to build something that matters over time.

This is not advice. It is a working document. The entries here are tested ideas, frameworks that have proven useful, and questions that remain open.

Principles Recorded So Far

On Focus

Attention is the primary resource. Everything else — time, effort, skill — is downstream of where attention goes. Guard it accordingly.

On Building

A project that ships imperfectly teaches more than a perfect project that never leaves the draft. The feedback loop between making and learning is the mechanism. Do not bypass it by planning indefinitely.

On Systems

A good system removes the need for willpower. If a behavior depends on daily motivation to sustain, it is not yet a system — it is a wish.
